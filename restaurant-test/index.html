J & Y Chinese Cuisine

Eating behavior plays a very important role in Chinese culture. "Book of Han" says, "The king regards the people as his heaven, and the people regard food as their heaven." The "Eight Policies of Hong Fan" in "Book of Zhou" puts "food" in the first place. The importance of "food" in Chinese culture can be seen. In China, food and drink have the same meaning as running a family, governing a country, and other major social affairs.

Scholar Zhang Guangzhi believes that “one of the best ways to reach the core of a culture is through its belly.” In Chinese culture, we can understand a national spirit through dietary behavior. One of the common greetings when Chinese people meet is "Have you eaten?" After a person is born, he first understands and grasps the world through "eating".
